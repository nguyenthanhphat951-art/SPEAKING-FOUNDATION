Lesson Aims

Lessons 8–9
In this lesson, you will master the 4-Stage Formula for describing a place in IELTS Part 2.
Typical IELTS Part 2 Questions
Describe a flat or house you would like to own one day.
Describe a shopping centre or mall you have visited.
Describe a place where people can do sports.
Describe a museum you have visited.
Describe a lake or river you know about or have seen.
4-Stage Formula
1
Say what it is and where it is
type of place + location prepositions
2
Say what it looks like
size, shape, appearance, atmosphere
3
Say what its purpose is
passive voice + past habits + popularity
4
Say why it is special and how you feel
zero/first conditional + feelings expressions

Stage 3: Passive Voice for Purpose

Stage 3
Use the passive voice to express the purpose or significance of a place.
Structure: be + past participle (+ by + agent)
The Pyramids of Egypt were constructed by unpaid workers.
Classical music is performed there every week.
This famous site is respected and admired by locals and tourists alike.
The cafe is mainly used for studying and relaxing.
Originally, the palace was built by the emperor as his private palace.
Useful Passive Phrases
is used for + verb-ing
was built by + agent
is/are painted in + colour
is surrounded by + noun
Popularity Expressions
be famous for + noun/verb-ing
be renowned for + noun/verb-ing
be popular with + types of people
be great for / excellent for + verb-ing

Stage 3: Expressing Past Habits

Stage 3
Use these structures to describe past habits at the place. Click any card to see an example.
Past Simple (with adverbs of frequency)
I visited the river at weekends and went swimming.
I always visited the river at weekends and went swimming.
Used to (for past states/habits that no longer happen)
I used to spend many hours painting by the river.
My family used to spend every summer camping at Giang Dien Waterfall.
When I was in high school, I used to go there quite regularly.
Would (for repeated past actions)
If I felt stressed, I would go to the bar to relax.
When I was a student, I would go there all the time and hang out with my friends.
Sometimes we would rent a boat and explore the area.

Stage 4: Zero & First Conditionals

Stage 4
Use conditionals to talk about habits and preferences related to the place.
Zero Conditional (always true)
If/when + present verb, present verb
If I am hungry, I go to a restaurant.
When I need a place to relax, I immediately think of the Summer Palace.
Whenever I feel overwhelmed, I tend to go there.
First Conditional (future possibility)
if + present verb, will + verb
If I have enough time, I will go shopping.
If I am very successful in my career, this is the kind of flat I will buy.
If I want to have a good time, I just need to go to D22.
Key Pattern: "Whenever I [feel/need/want]..., I [go/tend to/immediately]..." — This zero conditional pattern sounds very natural and native-like in IELTS Part 2.

Stage 4: Expressions for How You Feel

Stage 4
Use these expressions to explain why the place is special and how it makes you feel. Click any card to see an example.
I like/love (the place) because...
I love the cafe near my house because the food is so great!
I value (the place) because...
I really value this place as it gives me a sense of calm.
(The place) is important to me because...
The Summer Palace is really important to me because it is so tranquil.
(The place) makes me feel...
The British Museum always makes me feel inspired to learn more.
(The place) reminds me of... / brings back memories of...
It brings back memories of my school days, which makes it even more meaningful.
(The place) provides me with... / gives me... / lets me...
The park provides me with a pleasant, green space in which to play sports.

Model Answer 1: A Place You Like to Visit

Sample Q&A
Describe a place you like to visit.
Stage 1: What it is & where it is
I would like to talk about a small cafe near my house, which is located in the city centre, right next to a quiet park. I discovered this place a few years ago when I was looking for somewhere peaceful to study.
Stage 2: What it looks like
The cafe has a cosy and slightly dim atmosphere, with soft lighting and low background music, which makes it feel very relaxing. In terms of design, it has a modern but simple layout, with wooden tables, large glass windows, and neutral colours. The walls are painted in light tones, so the space feels bright and open.
Stage 3: Purpose
The cafe is mainly used for studying and relaxing, and it is particularly popular with students who need a quiet place to work. When I was in high school, I used to go there quite regularly, and sometimes I would just sit there for hours revising or reading.
Stage 4: Why it is special & how you feel
I really value this place as it gives me a sense of calm that is quite hard to find in the city. Whenever I feel overwhelmed, I tend to go there, and it helps me clear my head and focus again. It also brings back memories of my school days, which makes it even more meaningful to me.

Model Answer 2: A Natural Place

Sample Q&A
Describe a natural place you like to visit.
Stage 1
I would like to talk about a lake called Tuyen Lam Lake, which is located on the outskirts of Da Lat, surrounded by forests and hills. I first went there when I was in high school, and it quickly became one of my favourite places.
Stage 2
The lake is absolutely vast, with a wide stretch of calm water that reflects the sky beautifully. It is surrounded by dense pine forests and rolling hills, which makes the whole area feel fresh and untouched. What makes it even more interesting is that the place can feel slightly misty and mysterious, especially early in the morning.
Stage 3
The lake is mainly used for tourism and outdoor activities, and it is especially popular with people who enjoy nature. When I was younger, I used to go there quite often for picnics, and sometimes we would rent a boat and explore the area.
Stage 4
I am really attached to this place as it allows me to disconnect from everything else. Whenever I need a break, I go there, and I always feel more relaxed and refreshed afterwards. It is one of those places that helps me reset mentally, which is why it means so much to me.

Grammar Focus: used to vs would

Grammar
Both "used to" and "would" describe past habits, but they have different uses.
used to: states AND habits
I used to live near the lake. (state)
I used to go there every weekend. (habit)
The palace used to be closed to the public. (state)
would: habits ONLY (not states)
If I felt stressed, I would go to the bar. (habit)
Sometimes we would rent a boat. (habit)
I would live near the lake. (WRONG — state)
Natural Combination
When I was in high school, I used to go there quite regularly, and sometimes I would just sit there for hours revising or reading.
Key Rule: Use "used to" for both states and habits. Use "would" only for repeated actions/habits — never for states like "live", "be", "have", "know".

IELTS Part 2 Tips

IELTS Tips
Follow these tips to maximise your score in IELTS Speaking Part 2 place descriptions.
DO
Follow all 4 stages in order
Use specific location prepositions
Include personal memories (I remember...)
Use passive voice for purpose (is used for)
Use zero conditional for feelings (Whenever I...)
Speak for the full 1-2 minutes
DO NOT
Skip any of the 4 stages
Just list adjectives without examples
Use "would" for states (would live = WRONG)
Forget to explain why it is special
Memorise answers word for word
Stop before the time is up
