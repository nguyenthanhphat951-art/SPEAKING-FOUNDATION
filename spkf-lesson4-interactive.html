Model Answer 1: First time using a computer

Sample Q&A
When was the first time you used a computer?
The first time I used a computer was around my 10th birthday. I mainly used it to play games and learn some basic skills. At that time, everything felt new to me, so it was quite exciting and fascinating.
Time: around my 10th birthday
Purpose: to play / to learn
Linker: so
Adjectives: exciting / fascinating

Sample Q&A

Adjectives: enjoyable / relaxing

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Error Correction
Incorrect
"I go on holiday last summer." (wrong tense — should be went)
"I goed to Sapa." (goed is not a word — went)
"It was very good." (too vague — use specific adjectives)
"I went on holiday." (no time expression, no reason, no description)
Correct
"The last time I went on holiday was last summer." (correct past tense)
"My family and I went to Sapa." (correct irregular past tense)
"It was really enjoyable and relaxing." (specific adjectives)
"The last time I went on holiday was last summer. I went there to relax. It was really enjoyable."
Key Rules: (1) Always use past tense verbs. (2) Learn irregular verbs — went, came, had, saw, etc. (3) Use specific adjectives, not just "good" or "nice". (4) Follow the 3-step formula: When + Why + What it was like.

Grammar Focus: Past Simple Rules

Grammar
Master the rules for forming the past simple tense.
Regular Verbs: add -ed
watch → watched
visit → visited
stay → stayed
decide → decided
celebrate → celebrated
Irregular Verbs: must memorise
go → went (NOT goed)
come → came (NOT comed)
have → had (NOT haved)
feel → felt (NOT feeled)
become → became (NOT becomed)
Negative: did not + base form
I did not go. (NOT I did not went.)
She did not come. (NOT She did not came.)

IELTS Speaking Tips for Part 1

IELTS Tips
Follow these tips to maximise your score in IELTS Speaking Part 1 past experience questions.
DO
Always use a clear time expression first
Use correct past tense verbs (went, not goed)
Give a reason (to relax / because I wanted to)
Use specific adjectives (fascinating, not just good)
Use linkers: so, which, because, as
Speak for 3-5 sentences per answer
DO NOT
Use present tense for past events
Say "goed", "comed", "haved" (wrong forms)
Just say "It was good" (too vague)
Forget to say when it happened
Give one-word answers
Memorise answers word for word
Remember the Formula!
When (time expression) + Why (purpose/reason) + What it was like (adjective of experience)
