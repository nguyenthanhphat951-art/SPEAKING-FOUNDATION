In this lesson, you will master the 4-Stage Formula for describing a person in IELTS Part 2.
Typical IELTS Part 2 Questions
Describe a teacher you know.
Describe a person you enjoy spending time with.
Describe a famous person you would like to meet.
Describe a neighbour you have or have had.
Describe one of your friends.
4-Stage Formula
1
Say who they are and how you know them
relatives, occupations, relative clauses with who
2
Say what they are like and what they do
personality adjectives, adverbs of frequency
3
Say what they have achieved and how they influenced you
third conditional, present perfect, achievement phrases
4
Say why they are special and how you feel
admiration expressions, imitation expressions

Stage 1: Relative Clauses with WHO

Stage 1
Use relative clauses with who to add information about the person. There are two types.
Non-defining (extra information)
Adds extra info. Remove it and the sentence still makes sense. Use commas.
My mother, who worked as a waitress, thoroughly dislikes politics.
My grandmother, who is my father s mother, had to look after me.
Slight pause before and after the clause when speaking.
Defining (necessary information)
Identifies which person. Remove it and the sentence loses meaning. No commas.
The woman who lived next door to us when I was a child was extremely kind.
The movie star who starred in Titanic has become hugely successful.
No pause. Speak continuously through the clause.

Stage 1: Past Continuous & How You Met

Stage 1
Use the past continuous + past simple to explain how or when you met the person.
Structure: was/were + verb-ing (longer action) + past simple (shorter action)
When I was studying at university, my teacher told me to spend more time researching.
While my brother was visiting Vietnam, he lost his passport, wallet, and credit cards.
We met when we were working together at the same company.
I first talked to my girlfriend when I asked her the time while we were waiting for the bus.
When I was choosing my courses, I decided to try Roman History and so met Dr. Shotter.
Key Linkers: when / while / as / during the time that — all used to connect the past continuous with the past simple.

Stage 3: The Third Conditional

Stage 3
Use the third conditional to explain how the person influenced you — what would have been different without them.
Structure: if + had(n't) + past participle + would(n't) have + past participle
If it had not been for her, I would never have studied nursing, which I love.
I am sure I would not have studied a Master s degree if I had not met Dr. Shotter.
If my teacher had not been so relaxed, I am sure I would have studied harder.
I do not think I would be as outgoing if I had not spent so much time with him.
Things that did NOT happen
If my grandmother had not looked after me as a child, a babysitter would have.
Things that might not have happened
If my niece had not been so lazy at school, she would not have failed all her exams.

Stage 3: Present Perfect & Achievement Phrases

Stage 3
Use the present perfect to talk about the person's achievements and their impact on you.
Structure: have/has + past participle
My grandfather has had a great influence on my life.
She has become one of the most famous actresses in the world.
He has inspired a lot of students, including me, to become more interested in history.
She has helped me become more confident, especially in social situations.

Stage 4: Expressions of Imitation

Stage 4
Use these expressions to say you want to be like the person in some way.
I hope I am as + adjective + as + person
I hope I am as successful as Bill Gates.
I hope I will be as kind a teacher as Mrs. Capp.
I also hope that I am as good a grandmother as mine was.
I would love to be like...
I would love to be like my mother when I grow up.
I would like to be as... as...
I would like to be as well respected as my grandfather.
I want to be like...
I want to be like my neighbour Doris when I am older.
From the Model Answers
Gran was a great role model for me when I was growing up, and now I am an adult I still admire her.
I also hope that in the future, I can be as dedicated and inspiring as he is.
I would also love to be as confident and positive as him.

Model Answer 1: A Teacher

Sample Q&A
Describe a teacher you know.
Stage 1: Who they are
I would like to talk about my history teacher, Mr. Nam, who taught me in high school. I first got to know him when I was choosing my subjects, and he quickly became my favourite teacher.
Stage 2: What they are like
He is extremely knowledgeable, but what really stands out is how clearly he explains things. Instead of just giving definitions, he tends to break complex ideas down into simple steps. He is also very enthusiastic about his subject. He often brings lessons to life by adding real historical stories. Another thing I really appreciate is that he is incredibly supportive. He would stay behind after class to go over things again.
Stage 3: Achievements & influence
He has inspired a lot of students, including me, to become more interested in history. To be honest, I probably would not have chosen this subject if I had not been taught by him.
Stage 4: Why special & feelings
He is someone I really look up to, mainly because of his passion for teaching. I also hope that in the future, I can be as dedicated and inspiring as he is.

Model Answer 2: A Best Friend

Sample Q&A
Describe a person you enjoy spending time with.
Stage 1
I would like to talk about my best friend, Minh, who I met at school. We became close when we were studying together for exams, and we have stayed close ever since.
Stage 2
Minh is naturally funny, but not in an obvious way. He has a quick, subtle sense of humour, and he often comes up with jokes at just the right moment. He is also very outgoing and approachable, so he finds it easy to connect with people. He tends to start conversations naturally, even in unfamiliar situations. What I value most, though, is how supportive he is. Whenever I am dealing with something difficult, he is the kind of person who will sit and listen properly, rather than just giving quick advice.
Stage 3
He has helped me become more confident, especially in social situations. I do not think I would be as outgoing as I am now if I had not spent so much time with him.
Stage 4
He is really important to me, and I genuinely value our friendship. I would also love to be as confident and positive as him.

Grammar Focus: Third Conditional Rules

Grammar
Master the third conditional for talking about how someone influenced you.
Structure 1: if + had(n't) + pp, would(n't) have + pp
If it had not been for her, I would never have studied nursing.
If I had not met Dr. Shotter, I would not have studied a Master s degree.
Structure 2: would(n't) have + pp + if + had(n't) + pp
I would never have studied nursing if it had not been for her.
I would not have studied a Master s degree if I had not met Dr. Shotter.
Common Mistakes
If she did not help me, I would not pass. (WRONG — this is second conditional)
If she had not helped me, I would not passed. (WRONG — missing have)
If she had not helped me, I would not have passed. (CORRECT)

IELTS Part 2 Tips

IELTS Tips
Follow these tips to maximise your score in IELTS Speaking Part 2 person descriptions.
DO
Follow all 4 stages in order
Use relative clauses with who
Include personal stories and memories
Use the third conditional for influence
Use present perfect for achievements
Speak for the full 1-2 minutes
DO NOT
Skip any of the 4 stages
Just list adjectives without examples
Use second conditional instead of third
Forget to explain why they are special
Memorise answers word for word
Stop before the time is up
Remember the 4-Stage Formula!

Grammar Focus: Relative Clauses Summary

Grammar
Master the two types of relative clauses for IELTS Part 2.
Non-defining: extra info (use commas)
My grandmother, who is my father s mother, had to look after me.
My teacher, who taught me in high school, was very inspiring.
My friend Minh, who I met at school, is very supportive.
Defining: necessary info (no commas)
The woman who lived next door was extremely kind.
The teacher who inspired me most was Mr. Nam.
The friend who helped me through difficult times is Minh.
Common Mistakes
My grandmother which is my father s mother... (WRONG — use who for people)
My grandmother, who is my father s mother had to look after me. (WRONG — missing comma after clause)
My grandmother, who is my father s mother, had to look after me. (CORRECT)
