Native Speaker Words

Vocabulary
Learn these words and expressions to make your answers sound more natural and fluent.
generally speaking
In most situations; usually. "Generally speaking, I don't eat fast food."
regularly
Frequently; often. "Steve exercises regularly."
as for
Used to change the topic. "I love golf. As for cooking, I have no real interest."
as a rule
Usually; generally true. "As a rule, I'm a very calm person."
tend (to)
To be likely to happen; to usually do something. "Bikes tend to be popular with older people."
pop music
The most popular music in a society. "Some modern pop music is fantastic."
folk music
A traditional style of music, often from the countryside. "Folk music is considered old-fashioned by many young people."
depend on
Whether something is true is determined by something else. "It depends on what kind of holiday you want."

Model Answer: Is Football Popular?

Sample Q&A 1
Question: Is football popular in your country?
Generally speaking, I would say that the majority of high school and university male students regularly play football. As for watching football, I think most Vietnamese men of all ages frequently watch English Premier League matches. I don't believe football is very popular with many Vietnamese women, though.
People: male students, men of all ages, Vietnamese women
Quantity: the majority of, most, many
Habit: generally speaking, regularly, frequently
Contrast: as for, though

Model Answer: Is Music Popular?

Sample Q&A 2
Question: Is music popular in your country?
As a rule, the younger generation usually listens to pop, rock or hip-hop music, whereas the older people tend to listen to older Vietnamese songs or folk music. I think many wealthy people listen to classical music, sometimes going to live concerts. Most people in Vietnam listen to music every day.
People: younger generation, older people, wealthy people
Quantity: many, most
Habit: as a rule, usually, tend to
Contrast: whereas

Model Answer: Are Bikes Popular?

Sample Q&A 3
Question: Are bikes popular in your country?
Yes, I would say bikes are still quite popular, though it depends on the group of people. Generally speaking, the majority of school kids and students ride bikes to school or university because it's cheap and convenient. As for the older generation, many elderly people also tend to cycle in the early morning for exercise. However, very few wealthy people or office workers use bikes — they usually prefer cars or taxis.
People: school kids, students, elderly people, wealthy people, office workers
Quantity: the majority of, many, very few
Habit: generally speaking, tend to
Contrast: as for, however

Model Answer: Is Watching Team Sports Popular?

Sample Q&A 4
Question: Is watching team sports popular where you live?
Yes, absolutely. As a rule, the majority of men of all ages enjoy watching team sports, especially football. Most young men tend to gather with friends at cafés or sports bars to watch matches together. As for women, not many are into watching sports, though some do enjoy it. In general, football is by far the most popular sport to watch in my country.
People: men of all ages, young men, women
Quantity: the majority of, most, not many
Habit: as a rule, tend to, in general
Emphasis: by far the most popular

Model Answer: Is Cooking Popular?

Sample Q&A 5
Question: Is cooking popular in your country?
It depends on the group of people, really. Generally speaking, the majority of housewives and older women tend to cook at home every day, as it's a deeply rooted tradition. As for the younger generation, very few young people cook regularly — most of them prefer eating out or ordering food online because it's more convenient. However, a small percentage of young men and women are really into cooking as a hobby.
💡 Opener: "It depends on the group of people, really." — A great way to start when the answer varies by group!

Grammar: Contrast Words

Grammar Point
Use these words to compare different groups of people — they make your speech more natural and sophisticated.
whereas
Contrast within one sentence
"The younger generation listens to pop music, whereas the older people tend to prefer folk music."
however
Contrast at sentence start
"However, very few wealthy people or office workers use bikes."
as for
Shift focus to a new group
"As for women, not many are into watching sports."
though
Contrast at sentence end
"I don't believe football is very popular with many Vietnamese women, though."
Key Tip: Aim to use at least one contrast word per answer to show you can discuss different groups effectively.

Grammar: Giving Reasons

Grammar Point
Use these connectors to explain why groups of people behave differently.
because
Direct reason
"Most students ride bikes because it's cheap and convenient."
Placed directly before the reason clause. Most common in spoken English.
as
Formal connector
"Housewives cook every day, as it's a deeply rooted tradition."
More formal. Often placed mid-sentence after a comma.
probably because
Inferred reason
"Gardening is popular with elderly people, probably because it is therapeutic."
Used when the reason is not certain — shows an educated guess.

How to Build a Great Answer

5-Step Strategy
Step 1
Habit Expression
Open with a general statement phrase.
"Generally speaking…" / "As a rule…"
Step 2
Specific Group
Mention a particular type of person.
"the majority of students…" / "most elderly people…"
Step 3
Quantity Word
Add a word showing how many.
"most / very few / a lot of…"
Step 4
Give a Reason
Explain why using a reason clause.
"because it's cheap…" / "as it's convenient…"
Step 5
Contrast Group
Add a contrasting group for balance.
"However, very few wealthy people…"
Full Example: "Generally speaking, the majority of school kids ride bikes because it's cheap. However, very few wealthy people use bikes since they prefer to drive cars."

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Error Correction
Over-Generalising — Incorrect
"All people in my country like football."
"Vietnamese people listen to music."
"Nobody plays golf."
"Everyone rides bikes."
Specific & Qualified — Correct
"The majority of young men tend to watch football."
"Generally speaking, the younger generation usually listens to pop music."
"Not many women play golf in Vietnam."
"A lot of school kids ride bikes to school."
Avoid: all / nobody / everyone Use: the majority of / not many / a lot of Add: types of people Add: expressions of habit
