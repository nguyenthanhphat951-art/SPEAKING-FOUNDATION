Step 1: Adverbs of Frequency (Position Rules)

Language Step 1
The position of adverbs of frequency depends on the adverb. Click any card to see an example.
Before the verb only: always, rarely, never
always
I always read the Sunday papers.
rarely
Jonathan rarely goes to fast food restaurants.
never
I never read the Sunday papers.
Before the verb OR at the end: often, frequently
often
I often listen to music.  /  I listen to music often.
frequently
I frequently go to bars.  /  I go to bars frequently.
Beginning, before verb, OR end: usually, sometimes, occasionally
usually
Usually I play football.  /  I usually play football.  /  I play football usually.
sometimes
Sometimes I go.  /  I sometimes go.  /  I go sometimes.
occasionally
Occasionally Lee reads.  /  Lee occasionally reads.  /  Lee reads occasionally.
End of sentence only: all the time
all the time
I listen to my MP3 all the time.

Step 2: Colloquial Expressions of Infrequency

Language Step 2
Use these native-speaker expressions to sound more natural. They replace never, seldom, rarely and sometimes. Click any card to see the example.
every once in a while = sometimes
I play football on Sunday morning every once in a while.
from time to time = occasionally
I have friends over from time to time.
once in a blue moon = rarely
I drink alcohol once in a blue moon.
hardly ever = almost never
I hardly ever play video games.
Quick Reference: Formal vs Colloquial
sometimes → every once in a while
occasionally → from time to time
rarely → once in a blue moon
almost never → hardly ever

Model Answer 1: How often do you socialise?

Sample Q&A
How often do you socialise with friends?
I used to go out to dinner with my friends all the time, but I am in my last year of university now and I am really busy. I do not often have the chance to take a break and spend time with my friends. Once in a while, I will have someone over for coffee, or I will go to a friend house and hang out. I hope I will have more time to socialise after I graduate!
Frequency: used to / do not often / once in a while
Reason: busy with university
Extension: I hope I will have more time...
Answer Structure
Frequency + Reason + Extension/Detail

Model Answer 2: How often do you read books?

Sample Q&A
How often do you read books?
To be honest, I am always reading books. I usually read novels, but sometimes I mix it up slightly and read books about history or nature. I prefer reading novels because they are a great way to relax and escape from daily life, but I also enjoy history and nature books because they help me learn more about the world we live in. From time to time I might read some poetry, but I have to be in the right mood.
Frequency: always, usually, sometimes, from time to time
Reason: because they are a great way to relax
Extension: I have to be in the right mood

Model Answer 3: How often do you go out with friends?

Sample Q&A
How often do you go out with friends?
I often go out with my friends, mainly because it is a good way to unwind and catch up. We usually go to a cafe at the weekend, and it is a chance for us to talk about what has been going on in our lives.
Frequency: often, usually
Reason: unwind / catch up
Extension: talk about what has been going on

Model Answer 4: How often do you exercise?

Sample Q&A
How often do you exercise?
I always exercise in the morning because it helps me stay healthy and feel more energetic. I usually go jogging in the park, and it really helps me start the day feeling more positive and focused.
Frequency: always, usually
Reason: stay healthy / feel more energetic
Extension: start the day feeling more positive and focused

Model Answer 5: How often do you watch TV?

Sample Q&A
How often do you watch TV?
I hardly ever watch TV because I am usually busy with my studies. I tend to spend my free time reading or doing homework instead, especially when I have exams coming up.
Infrequency: hardly ever
Reason: busy with studies
Extension: I tend to... / especially when...

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Error Correction
Incorrect
"I read books." (no frequency word at all)
"Always I go to the gym." (always cannot start a sentence)
"I go always to the gym." (always must be before the verb)
"I read books. I like them." (no reason given)
Correct
"I usually read books in the evening because it helps me relax."
"I always go to the gym." (always before the verb)
"Usually I go to the gym." (usually can start a sentence)
"I often read books because they help me relax and escape from daily life."
Key Rules: (1) Always use a frequency word. (2) always/rarely/never go BEFORE the verb only. (3) usually/sometimes/occasionally are flexible. (4) Always give a reason with because/since/as.

Grammar Focus: Adverb Position Rules

Grammar
Master the exact position rules for each adverb of frequency.
FIXED position — before the verb ONLY

Usually I play football.    I usually play football.    I play football usually.

I listen to my MP3 all the time.    (NOT: I all the time listen...)

Extension Phrases: Deep Dive

Language Focus
Use these extension phrases to add extra detail and sound more natural. Click any card to see a full example.
I tend to...
Used to describe a habit or pattern. More natural than "I usually".
I tend to read for a while before going to bed, which also helps me sleep better.
especially when...
Adds a specific condition or situation that makes the habit more or less frequent.
I hardly ever watch TV, especially when I have exams coming up.
I hope I will...
Adds a future aspiration — shows you can talk about future plans too.
I hope I will have more time to socialise after I graduate!
It is a chance for me/us to...
Explains the social or personal benefit of the activity.
We usually go to a cafe, and it is a chance for us to talk about what has been going on in our lives.
