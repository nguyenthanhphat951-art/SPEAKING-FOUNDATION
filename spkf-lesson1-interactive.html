Step 1: Synonyms for LIKE

Language Step 1
Instead of always saying "I like", use these synonyms. Click any card to see the example sentence.
enjoy + verb-ing
I really enjoy listening to classical music in my free time.
love + verb-ing
She loves trying new cuisines when traveling abroad.
be fond of + verb-ing
He is quite fond of painting landscapes.
be into + noun/verb-ing
My brother is really into sci-fi movies.
be keen on + noun/verb-ing
She is keen on learning new languages.
adore + verb-ing
Kids absolutely adore playing with puppies.
be a fan of + noun
I am a big fan of K-pop music.
take pleasure in + verb-ing
He takes pleasure in gardening on the weekends.
Degree Adverbs: Use really, quite, absolutely, particularly, especially to express how much you like something.

Model Answer 1: Do you like listening to music?

Sample Q&A
Do you like listening to music?
Yes, absolutely. I really enjoy listening to music, mainly because it helps me relax after a long day at school. It is a great way to unwind and clear my head. I am especially into pop music, so I often listen to it while doing homework, which helps me stay focused.
Like expressions: really enjoy / be into
Reason linker: mainly because / so
Benefit phrases: helps me relax / unwind / clear my head / stay focused
Answer Structure
Yes/No + Synonym + Reason linker + Benefit phrase + Example/Detail

Model Answer 2: Do you like doing housework?

Sample Q&A
Do you like doing housework?
No, not really. I am not really into doing housework because it is quite boring and sometimes exhausting. It does not really help me relax, either. For example, when I have to clean my room, I usually feel tired afterwards, so I just want to rest.
Dislike expressions: not really / not into
Reason linker: because / so
Drawbacks: boring / exhausting / tired
Negative structure: does not really help me + verb

Model Answer 3: Do you enjoy studying English?

Sample Q&A
Do you enjoy studying English?
Yes, I do. I am quite keen on studying English because it allows me to learn new things and become more confident when communicating. It also gives me the chance to meet different people. For instance, I sometimes talk to foreign teachers in class, which really helps improve my speaking skills.
Like expression: keen on
Reason linker: because
Educational benefits: learn new things / become more confident / meet different people
Structures: allows me to + verb / gives me the chance to + verb

Model Answer 4: Do you like watching TV?

Sample Q&A
Do you like watching TV?
Yes, I suppose so. I am quite a fan of watching TV, especially when I want to relax after studying. It is a good way to forget about daily stress and just enjoy some free time. I usually watch comedy shows in the evening, and they always make me feel more relaxed.
Like expression: a fan of
Reason linker: especially when
Recreational benefits: relax / forget stress / feel more relaxed
Structure: It is a good way to + verb

Model Answer 5: Do you like waking up early?

Sample Q&A
Do you like waking up early?

For example, when I wake up early for school, I often feel sleepy and find it hard to concentrate, so I prefer waking up later when I can.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Error Correction
Incorrect
"Yes. I like music." (too short, no reason or example)
"I like music because I like it." (repetitive, no real reason)
"I enjoy to listen to music." (wrong: enjoy + verb-ing, not to + verb)
"I like. I like. I like." (no synonyms, repetitive)
Correct
"Yes, absolutely. I really enjoy listening to music because it helps me relax after a long day."
"I am keen on music because it gives me the chance to express my feelings."
"I enjoy listening to music." (correct: enjoy + verb-ing)
"I enjoy listening... I am also fond of watching movies." (varied synonyms)
Grammar Rule: enjoy / love / hate / adore / cannot stand + verb-ing (NOT to + verb). E.g. "I enjoy listening" NOT "I enjoy to listen".

Grammar Focus: enjoy/hate + verb-ing

Grammar
These verbs are always followed by verb-ing, NOT "to + verb". This is one of the most common mistakes!
Always + verb-ing
enjoy listening to music
love traveling abroad
hate waking up early
cannot stand doing housework
adore playing with puppies
take pleasure in gardening
NEVER + to + verb
enjoy to listen to music
love to travel abroad
hate to wake up early
cannot stand to do housework
adore to play with puppies
take pleasure in to garden
Exception: "would like" and "would love" ARE followed by "to + verb". E.g. "I would like to travel to Japan." vs "I love traveling to Japan."

Speaking Tips for IELTS Part 1

IELTS Tips
Follow these tips to maximise your score in IELTS Speaking Part 1.
DO
Answer Yes/No clearly first
Use synonyms to vary your vocabulary
Give at least one reason using because/since
Add a specific example or personal detail
Speak for 3-5 sentences per answer
Use degree adverbs: really, quite, absolutely
DO NOT
Give one-word answers (Yes. / No.)
Repeat "I like" without synonyms
Say "enjoy to + verb" (wrong grammar)
Give reasons without examples
Speak too fast or too slowly
Memorise answers word for word
